Events

  1. High card
    • High card wins
  2. Pint race
    • The draw of cards will decide the drink:
      • Ace = Ale
      • 2 = Lager
      • 3 = Cider
      • 4 = Guinness
    • Spills/dregs = disqualification
  3. Wrong arm throw
    • Throw with non-dominant hand
    • Furthest throw wins
    • Order dictated by the draw of cards, highest first, lowest last
    • Shuffling up the field / stepping over the throwing line = disqualification.
  4. Gladiator duel
    • Knock your opponent off the horizontal telgraph pole with 'pugil sticks'
    • We will draw positions with cards in a duelling tournament
    • Leaders journey helmet may be removed
  5. Hill Climb Time Trial
    • Hurtle up an inclined section of a quiet country lane
    • A sturdy work horse with ‘extra chunky’ steel frame
    • Quickest wins
  6. Straw-peado Relay
    • Two captains, randomly selected
    • A relay race the first team to finish will take home the glory, receiving all the available points on the board split between evenly them
  7. Russian roulette penalties
    • The Goalie will be the competitor with the lowest card, the highest card stepping up first
    • Taking turns, if you score first you win. If you miss the goal (put it wide) you head to the back of the line
    • If the goal keeper saves a penalty and holds onto the ball (catches/grabs the ball) the keeper takes first place in the cue
    • The player who’s penalty was saved and held goes in goal.
  8. Sneeze the fastest
    • Taking it in turns in a time trialled event from a standing start to sneeze the fastest.
    • The sneeze must be genuine, and to bring on this nasal victory you may bring anything you wish to help you to sneeze quickest.
  9. Chug 100
    • The first to run 100m and completely down a can of beer will be the winner
    • It is the competitor’s choice if they run and drink, drink then run, or run then drink.
    • You may not cross the finish line until the can has been finished
  10. 2 Dares 1 Cup
    • Split into 4 teams
    • Play two simultaneous games of beer pong with only one cup on each side
    • There will be two dares under each cup, when an opponent sinks a ball into your cup, you draw one of the dares from under it
    • The player on your team who was last to throw must do the dare to keep the cup
    • Don’t do the dare, and you lose a lifeline, either do the other dare if there is one to keep the cup, or you are out
  11. Fives
    • Standard rules apply
  12. Non-tactical pub golf triathlon
    • Pub golf scoring
    • Highest number of points over 3 holes wins
      • -Pint of ale, bitter, lager, cider or Guinness = Par 2
      • Single spirit & mixer = Par 2
      • Double spirit & mixer = Par 3
      • Glass of wine = Par 3
      • Two of the same pint of ale, bitter, lager, cider or Guinness = Par 5
      • Two different pints of ale, bitter, lager, cider or Guinness = Par 6
      • Bottle of wine = par 10
    • Anyone caught chundering, either tactically or accidentally will be stripped of their points for this game (sorry Keith)
  13. Olympic torch Regatta
    • Double points
    • Race around the farmyard to a flaming beacon, the Olympic torch
    • The competitor must down a can of strong lager or cider (7%+)
    • Then eat a single dry cream cracker
    • They must present their empty mouth to the competitor behind them
    • At the fourth and last base, the competitor must cross the finish line and then grab the flaming torch

Rules

Jersey

The leaders Jersey this year will be worn by the leader from first thing on Saturday Morning, the full onesie and helmet must be worn. This helmet significantly impairs the wearers vision, and similar to Darth Maul in Shoreham, therefore will level the playfield. There will be no loser’s jersey this year, other than the look of abject disappointment that will inevitably overcome you should you hold this position.


Jokers

Joker rules once again apply. 1 joker per competitor. You must clearly announce that you wish to use your joker before the relevant round begins. Your joker will double your score for that round. Your Joker cannot be used for the Regatta.


Veto

This year we will introduce a Veto system, competitors will have 1 veto that can be used to stop a player from using their joker on a round. In the case that a competitor plays their joker, and has it vetoed by an opponent, the competitor can use their veto to keep their joker on the event. If vetoed you still have your Joker to use on a different round, there are no limits to the number of times one individual can be vetoed.

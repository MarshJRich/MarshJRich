Events

  1. Pint race
    • Straight race to finish a pint of beer or cider
    • Glass must start on the table and finishes upside down on ones head
  2. Worms
    • Randomly drawn semi-finals
    • Top two from each heat progress to final
    • Bottom two to battle for the few points that remain
  3. Gym test
    • Dips and squats thrusts
  4. 100m dash
    • Fastest wins
    • Shotgun your lane
  5. F1 racestars
    • Endurance championship
    • Semi-finals randomly drawn
    • Top two from each heat progress to final
    • Bottom two to battle for the few points that remain
  6. Strawpedo elimination race
    • Conventional strawpedo race
    • Slowest competitor is eliminated
    • Repeat
  7. Darts
    • Each contestant has 15 darts
    • Total score dictates order
  8. Team race
    • In teams of two
    • First: three legged race
    • Second: Piggy back there and back
    • Third: Wheelbarrow there and back
    • Teams swap round at each end
  9. Wrong arm throw
    • One attempt to throw football as far as possible
    • ...with the wrong arm
    • Stand where the football stops
  10. Regatta
    • Sprint to the designated point
    • Down a cup of K or special brew
    • Sprint to the next designated point
    • Eat 3 crackers
    • Sprint to the designated landmark
    • Spin around 10 times and race through the tunnel

Rules

Jersey

The leaders’ jersey this year is a wonderful combination of things geared primarily towards ensuring humility in the leader’s demeanour throughout, with a secondary goal of impeding performance to level the playing field.


Jokers

Standard joker rules apply again. 1 joker per Olympian. You must clearly announce you wish to use your joker before the relevant event begins. Your joker will double your points tally for that round. Your joker cannot be used on the Regatta.


Double or Nothing (DoN)

This year, when using their jokers, Olympians can also announce Double or Nothing (standard joker rules apply). As the name suggests this will either double the bonus points or completely nullify them. Double or nothing is decided by flip of a 50 pence coin (see appendix).

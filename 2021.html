Events

  1. Low card
    • Each Olympian draws 1 card
    • Lowest card wins
    • Ace is low.
  2. Pint race
    • First to down a pint wins. There’s just one twist…
    • This year, fellow Olympians, we will be downing pints of wine
    • In the deck there will be 2 Aces, 2 Twos, 2 Threes, and 2 Fours
      • Draw an Ace and you’ll be downing a pint of white wine
      • Draw a Two and you’ll be downing a pint of rose wine
      • Draw a Two and you’ll be downing a pint of rose wine
      • Draw a Three and you’ll be downing a pint of RED, RED WINE
      • Draw a Four and you’ll be downing a pint of sparkling wine
    • All wines must be a minimum of 11% ABV. The ABV % must be visible and part of the original label
    • Spills/dregs = disqualification.
  3. Carabao rock paper scissors cup
    • Each game will be decided by a best of 3
    • cycle through 12 match-ups
    • A card draw will decide the initial pairings
    • winners progressing to contest top spots and losers the bottom
    • Contestants must show on scissors i.e. rock paper scissors (show) rather than showing after scissors i.e. rock paper scissors then show
    • Symbols must be clear – no half way houses
  4. Speed chunder 3000
    • The event format is time trial
    • Olympians will take it in turns from a standing start to vomit the fastest into a toilet
    • Missing the toilet = disqualification + 10 point penalty on your running total. You must also clean up your mess immediately.
  5. 100m backwards dash
    • This years’ 100m sprint will be done running backwards
    • A pack of cards will determine which competitors race in which heat
    • Ace = heat 1
    • Two = heat 2
    • The two fastest Olympians from each heat will race again to decide the top 4 positions
    • The other competitors will race again to decide the remaining positions
    • False start = disqualification
    • Not running backwards = disqualification
  6. Through the leg wrong arm throw
    • Using their non-dominant arm, Olympians will take it in turns to throw an item as far as possible – through their legs
    • Throwing order will be dictated by the draw of cards; highest first, lowest last
    • Both feet must remain firmly planted on the floor throughout the throwing motion and after release until the thrower has gathered themselves and returned to a fully upright position.
    • The winner will be the competitor who flings the item furthest; measured in a straight line at 90 degrees to the flinging line.
    • Non-dominant = opposite to the hand you write with
  7. Inverse strawpedo
    • The event format is time trial
    • Olympians must get into a handstand position (with the support of 2 fellow contenders)
    • A third volunteer will be on straw duty – feeding it into the upside down Olympian’s gaping mouth
    • Timer starts on the timekeeper’s “go” and stops once the VK is empty
  8. Wall sit
    • Olympians must assume the wall sit position and hold it for as long as possible
    • Longest wall sit wins
    • The timer will start as soon as the correct position is assumed
    • Feet roughly shoulder width apart
    • Legs bent roughly 90 degrees at the knee so that thigh is parallel to the floor
  9. Endurion
    • Olympians must drink 1 shot of beer every 1 minute until they can’t anymore
    • Shots will be 50ml measures
    • If you vomit you are out
    • If you miss the timer by more than 2 seconds you are out
    • Last Olympian standing (more likely sitting or lying) wins
    • Tie breakers are decided by sudden death spirit shot rounds. Each contender can choose from Vodka, Gin, Tequila, or Whisky
  10. Game of Thrones Tens
    • Fives but with all 10 fingers and thumbs
    • Standard Fives rules apply and are adjusted for Tens
    • If the competitor guesses correctly, they are out and must say ‘Thank you very much for a lovely game of Tens’ without laughing, smiling or celebrating, and while looking other competitors in the eyes
    • Failure to do any of the above while saying ‘Thank you very much for a lovely game of Tens’ means you’re back in the game
    • First out = winner. Last remaining = loser
  11. Regatta 400
    • Olympians will start by downing a can of strong lager or cider (7%+)
    • They must then eat a single dry cream cracker at each of the 3 refuelling stations along the circuit; presenting their empty mouth to the competitor behind them before continuing on to the next station
    • In addition to the crackers, Olympians will each be given a lime and a hat (selected from offering in high card order)
    • Olympians will carry these with them throughout and must cross the finish line with them intact
    • At each base Olympians must successfully complete 3 keepie uppies with the lime. After the 3rd of these strikes they must catch the lime in their hat
    • Spills/dregs = disqualification
    • Broken/lost lime/hat = disqualification

Jersey

The leaders’ jersey this year is a wonderful combination of things geared primarily towards ensuring humility in the leader’s demeanour throughout, with a secondary goal of impeding performance to level the playing field.

  • The performance flops

Jokers

Standard joker rules apply again. 1 joker per Olympian. You must clearly announce you wish to use your joker before the relevant event begins. Your joker will double your points tally for that round. Your joker cannot be used on the Regatta.


Double or Nothing (DoN)

This year, when using their jokers, Olympians can also announce Double or Nothing (standard joker rules apply). As the name suggests this will either double the bonus points or completely nullify them. Double or nothing is decided by flip of a 50 pence coin (see appendix).
